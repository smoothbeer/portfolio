Overview

A lo-fi zine archiving Kanye West's tweets from January to March 2016 — right around the Life of Pablo rollout. Photocopied, hand-bound with pink thread, and produced in a single run.

Made at 8 Ball Community, an artist collective operating out of a basement in Soho, NYC. Part zine library, part radio station, part public access studio — a space where anyone could walk in, use the tools, and publish something. I was living in New York at the time and started making zines to learn the form and contribute to the collective's output.

This was pre-controversy Kanye. The tweets read as stream-of-consciousness dispatches — album title changes, Madison Square Garden sellouts, "I hope you have a nice weekend." Collected and printed, they become an accidental time capsule of a specific moment in internet culture.

Inside

Tweet screenshots cut and scattered over photocopied imagery — Yeezus-era press shots, album artwork, family photos. The collage treatment matches the tone of the tweets themselves: raw, unfiltered, jumping between subjects with no transition.
